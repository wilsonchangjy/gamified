Uber

The Uber app gives you the power to get where you want to go with access to different types of rides across more than 10,000 cities. Uber is a platform where those who drive and deliver can connect with riders, eaters, and restaurants. In cities where Uber is available, you can use the Uber app to request a ride.

    Uber is by far the most popular ridesharing service around, with 93 million active users across the globe in selected regions.


    However, the application used by Uber drivers is known to be quasi-Taylorist in nature thanks to the heavy use of gamification elements such as non-stop ride notifications that "conveniently" appear once a ride is reaching its destination, impossible-to-ignore progress bars that show just how close they are to reaching their weekly goals, and limited-time surge zones that promise a higher reward.


    This control the platform attempts to establish aims to make sure that their drivers are completing as many rides as possible. This possible "mistreatment" is made worse due to the fact that the drivers are not considered employees of Uber and won't such receive benefits.

    Gamification

    Remember!

    The concept of gamification is not about playing games, but rather centred around the motivations of your target audience.

    Goals

    Ask the Hard Questions

    Solidify the goals and objectives of your project clearly:

  • What actions and decisions does your target user need to take?
  • What are the barriers that are holding them back?
  • What kind of learning outcome is intended?

    Players

    Think Big!

    Although it is better to have a distinct player type in mind, it will be useful to balance out other motivations as to not alienate other members of the target audience.

    Inclusivity goes a long way when scaling up.

    Motivations

    What Keeps Us Going

    It is always better to motivate your target user in an authentic context, instead of tricking them to do what you think needs to happen.

    Would you rather want to do something, or need to do something?
